You Tap "Start Round." Now What?

Let's trace what happens under the hood when you play a round. This is the best way to understand an app — follow one concrete action from start to finish.

1
You tap "Start Round"

The AppShell routes you to the Course Select screen

2
You pick a course and tee box

The app pulls course data from the local Drift database

3
You enter scores hole-by-hole

Each score is saved instantly to Drift — no internet needed

4
You finish the round

The Handicap Engine recalculates your index

5
Your score syncs to the cloud

If you have signal, Supabase pushes it to the live leaderboard

The Score Badge System

ScoreCaddie uses the same color language golfers already know. Each score gets a badge that tells you at a glance how you did:

Eagle 2 under par — like hitting a home run in golf
Birdie 1 under par — the sweet spot every golfer chases
Par On target — the expected score for a skilled player
Bogey 1 over par — still perfectly respectable
Double Bogey 2 over par — the app caps scores here to keep handicaps fair
💡
Key Insight

That "Double Bogey" cap isn't a bug — it's the ESC cap, a rule from the official World Handicap System. The app enforces it automatically so your handicap stays fair.
