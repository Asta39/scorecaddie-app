How a voice becomes coaching advice in 12 seconds

Meet Daniel

Daniel isn't just a chatbot. He's a British-accented AI caddie who stands on the driving range with you, watches your shots, and tells you exactly what to fix. He lives inside the AICaddieService class and has a very specific personality:

🧠

Groq LLaMA 3.3 70B

The brain — analyzes your shot data and generates personalized coaching advice

🎙️

ElevenLabs TTS

The voice — converts text advice into spoken audio with a British accent

📡

SharedPreferences

The memory — remembers if you turned voice mode on or off

Daniel's system prompt tells him he's "standing on the driving range in Kenya" and to "detect patterns over 3+ shots." He doesn't just comment on one swing — he remembers your last few and spots trends. That's why the service passes recentShots alongside the current shot.

The Code Behind Daniel

Here's the actual code that makes Daniel work. The getCoachingFeedback method sends your shot data to Groq and returns text advice:

CODE 
static Future<String> getCoachingFeedback({
  required Map<String, dynamic> shot,
  required db.UserProfile player,
  required List<Map<String, dynamic>> recentShots,
}) async {
  final response = await http.post(
    Uri.parse(_groqChatUrl),
    headers: {
      'Authorization': 'Bearer $_groqApiKey',
      'Content-Type': 'application/json',
    },
    body: jsonEncode({
      'model': _groqModel,
      'temperature': 0.7,
      'max_tokens': 150,
      'messages': [
        {'role': 'system', 'content': _buildSystemPrompt(player)},
        {'role': 'user', 'content': 'CURRENT SHOT: $shot. RECENT: $recentShots'},
      ],
    }),
  ).timeout(const Duration(seconds: 12));

  if (response.statusCode != 200)
    throw Exception('Daniel is quiet right now.');

  final data = jsonDecode(response.body);
  return data['choices'][0]['message']['content'];
}
